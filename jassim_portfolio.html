No-Show Killer: Automation Pipeline

The Automation Pipeline

1
Zapier Webhook (Catch Hook)

Your Express backend sends a JSON payload. Zapier catches the client's name, contact info, service choice, and the unique NSK- booking ID.

2
Google Calendar (Create Event)

Zapier uses the date and time from the payload. It instantly blocks the slot on the calendar. This prevents double bookings.

3
Gmail (Send Email)

An HTML confirmation email fires off. The client gets their receipt and appointment details right in their inbox.

4
Formatter (Text Replace)

This step preps the phone number for the local network. It converts the +639 country code into the standard 09 format so the API accepts it.

5
Semaphore SMS (PH SMS API)

The system sends a POST request to Semaphore. A confirmation text hits the client's phone immediately.

Automated Lead Enrichment & Routing: Automation Pipeline

The Automation Pipeline

1
Youform (Catch the Lead)

The system captures the initial inquiry the moment a local shop submits your contact form.

2
Formatter & Webhooks (Data Enrichment)

Zapier cleans up the incoming text and uses a GET webhook to instantly pull in external business metrics, gauging the size and potential of the company.

3
Paths (Smart Routing)

The workflow evaluates the enriched data and automatically splits the lead into a High Priority or Low Priority track based on your specific scoring rules.

4
High Priority (Log, Alert, & AI Pitch)

If the shop fits your target profile, the system logs the lead into a Google Sheet database and pings the team in Slack. Then, it uses AI to draft and send a highly personalized outreach pitch directly to the owner.

5
Low Priority (Standard Sequence)

If the inquiry is from a smaller shop that doesn't meet the target metrics, the system automatically routes them to a standard email sequence, ensuring a professional response without burning your manual resources.
